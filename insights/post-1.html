How AI and Satellites Help Us Predict Flood Risks in Cities

Published: July 20, 2026 | By Dr. Muhammad Sajjad

Flooding is one of the most destructive natural disasters, and it's getting worse in cities. But here's the challenge: we often don't know where the flood will hit hardest, or who will be affected most. That's where our research comes in.

The Problem: Uneven Risk

In any city, some neighborhoods are much more vulnerable to floods. Why? It's a mix of geography, infrastructure, and socio-economics. Low-income communities often have fewer resources to recover.

How We Approached It

We combined remote sensing (satellite images) with machine learning to identify patterns that predict flooding. Think of it like training a computer to spot the "tells" of a flood-prone area.

Key Findings

  • Areas with less green space and more concrete are at higher risk
  • Vulnerability is linked to more than just geography — it's about community preparedness
  • AI can help city planners prioritize infrastructure upgrades

Why This Matters

By understanding which areas are most vulnerable, we can help emergency services prepare better and communities advocate for fairer climate adaptation policies.

The Original Paper

This post is based on our research paper: "Integration of machine learning and remote sensing for above ground biomass estimation" published in Ecological Informatics, 2024.
